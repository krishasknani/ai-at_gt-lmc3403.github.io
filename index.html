What is "Vibe Coding"?

Vibe coding is a term coined to describe a new approach to programming where developers rely heavily on AI tools like ChatGPT, GitHub Copilot, or Claude to generate code based on natural language descriptions. Rather than understanding the underlying logic, programmers describe what they want and let AI handle the implementation.

While this can boost productivity for experienced developers, it poses significant risks for students who skip the fundamental learning process entirely.

The Core Issue

When students bypass the struggle of problem-solving by outsourcing their thinking to AI, they miss out on developing critical cognitive skills. The temporary convenience of AI-generated answers leads to long-term deficits in understanding, retention, and the ability to apply knowledge in novel situations. We're creating a generation of students who can prompt but cannot think.

Student Body Survey

We're conducting a comprehensive, student body-wide survey to understand how Georgia Tech students currently use AI in their coursework. This data will help us develop guidelines that reflect the unique culture, workload, and academic rigor of our institution.

Assignment Classification Framework

N

Novelty

How new or unfamiliar is the material? Higher novelty assignments should restrict AI usage to ensure students grapple with new concepts firsthand.

R

Rigidity

How fixed are the requirements? Highly rigid assignments with specific correct answers may benefit from AI restrictions, while open-ended projects could allow more flexibility.

C

Complexity

What is the cognitive load required? Complex, multi-step problems should be protected learning opportunities where students develop systematic thinking skills.

Process-Oriented Grading

Traditional outcome-based grading incentivizes students to get the "right answer" by any means necessary. Our proposed process-oriented grading approach shifts focus to:

  • ‚úì Documenting problem-solving approaches and iterations
  • ‚úì Explaining reasoning behind design decisions
  • ‚úì Demonstrating understanding through verbal or written defenses
  • ‚úì Reflecting on mistakes and learning moments
  • ‚úì Tracking incremental progress through version control

By valuing the journey over the destination, we encourage genuine learning while still preparing students for a world where AI tools exist.

Why Georgia Tech-Specific Data Matters

Every institution has its own culture, expectations, and student mindset. What works at one university may not translate to another. Georgia Tech students face unique challenges:

  • Rigorous STEM-focused curriculum with demanding technical courses
  • High-pressure environment with competitive culture
  • Strong emphasis on innovation and practical application
  • Diverse student body with varying levels of prior experience

By gathering data specific to our community, we can create guidelines that acknowledge these realities while still prioritizing genuine learning outcomes.
